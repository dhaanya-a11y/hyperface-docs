# Introduction

Welcome to the **Offer Management** documentation.

The Offer Management module enables issuers to create, configure, publish, and manage customer reward campaigns through an intuitive step-by-step workflow. Depending on the business requirement, different offer types can be configured to reward customers for eligible transactions, spending milestones, merchant purchases, and other campaign objectives.

This documentation provides detailed guidance for configuring each offer type, including eligibility criteria, reward calculation, budgeting, display settings, communication, and publishing.

---

## Offer Types

Hyperface supports four offer types, each suited to a different reward strategy. Select the type that best matches your campaign objective, then follow its dedicated guide for full configuration instructions.

### Transactional Offer

A Transactional Offer rewards customers for a single qualifying transaction, evaluated independently against the configured eligibility criteria. It suits instant, one-time rewards tied to individual purchases — such as merchant-, MCC-, or MID-based cashback and reward point campaigns — where there is no need to track cumulative customer activity over time.

### Milestone Offer

A Milestone Offer rewards customers once they reach a predefined spending or transaction milestone within the offer period, based on cumulative activity rather than a single transaction. It works well for campaigns that encourage customers to build toward a spend or transaction-count target, with multiple milestone levels each unlocking their own reward.

### Merchant Offer

A Merchant Offer rewards customers for making qualifying purchases at specific participating merchants, with eligibility determined by merchant-specific criteria. It is the right choice for co-branded or partnership campaigns, or any promotion designed to drive spend at select partner merchants.

### Gamified Offer

A Gamified Offer rewards customers progressively as they cross multiple spending or transaction-count thresholds — or levels — within a defined period, unlocking a distinct reward at each level. It suits tiered spend campaigns and engagement programs built around game-like, level-by-level progression.

## Documentation Structure

Each offer guide follows a consistent step-by-step configuration journey, covering:

- Creating a new offer
- Configuring offer details
- Setting up the offer user base
- Defining eligibility conditions
- Configuring reward calculation
- Setting budgets and approvals
- Configuring display elements
- Setting up communication
- Reviewing and publishing the offer

---

## Before You Begin

Before creating an offer, ensure that:

- The required issuer and program are available.
- The appropriate reward outcome has been identified.
- Any required customer eligibility criteria are defined.
- The necessary permissions are available to create and publish offers.

<Note>

The available configuration steps may vary depending on the selected offer type.

</Note>
