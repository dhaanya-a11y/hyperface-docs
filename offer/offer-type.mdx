# Offer Type

## What is an Offer Type?

An **Offer Type** defines how a customer becomes eligible to receive a reward. It determines the business objective of an Offer and the qualification logic used to evaluate customer activities. Selecting the appropriate Offer Type is the first step in creating an Offer, as it defines the configuration journey and the business rules that will be applied.

Hyperface supports multiple Offer Types, allowing issuers to create campaigns for different business objectives.

![Offer Type Selection](/images/c.png)

---

## Transactional Offer

A **Transactional Offer** rewards customers whenever a single qualifying transaction satisfies the configured eligibility criteria. Each transaction is evaluated independently, making it suitable for campaigns where rewards should be issued immediately after every eligible transaction.

Typical business use cases include fuel cashback, dining offers, shopping rewards, and UPI cashback campaigns.

## Milestone Offer

A **Milestone Offer** rewards customers after they achieve predefined spending or transaction milestones within a specified period. Rewards are issued only after the configured milestone conditions are successfully completed.

Typical business use cases include monthly spending campaigns, spend-and-earn rewards, transaction count campaigns, and customer retention programs.

## Merchant Offer

A **Merchant Offer** rewards customers for making purchases at participating merchants. Eligibility is determined using merchant-specific criteria, making it suitable for targeted merchant campaigns and partner promotions.

Typical business use cases include restaurant cashback, shopping offers, travel promotions, and exclusive merchant partnerships.

![Merchant Offer](/images/1.png)

👉 [Merchant Offer Configuration](/merchant-offer/introduction)

## Gamified Offer

A **Gamified Offer** rewards customers through interactive campaigns, challenges, or engagement-based activities. Customers unlock rewards after completing predefined objectives, making campaigns more engaging and encouraging repeat participation.

Typical business use cases include spin-the-wheel campaigns, scratch cards, seasonal promotions, and customer engagement challenges.
