---
title: Outcome Type
description: Configure the reward or benefit customers receive after meeting the offer conditions.
---
An **Outcome Type** defines the reward or benefit that a customer receives after successfully meeting an offer's eligibility criteria. It determines the type of benefit that is issued once the configured offer conditions are fulfilled.

Outcome Type is one of the first choices made during the offer creation journey, immediately after selecting the **Offer Type**. During offer creation, select the Outcome Type that best aligns with your campaign objective.

The selected Outcome Type establishes the type of value delivered to the customer—whether it is money, reward points, a voucher, a Smart Tag, or another supported reward. This selection influences the subsequent reward configuration steps.

<Note>
The selected **Outcome Type** affects the configuration options available in **Offer Calculation** and **Posting**. Different outcome types require different reward and posting configurations. For example, a **Smart Tag** outcome does not require a monetary reward calculation like **Cashback** or **Reward Points**.
</Note>

## Comparing Outcome Types

| Consideration | Cashback | Reward Points | Voucher | Smart Tag | Accelerated Reward | Partner Points |
|---------------|----------|---------------|----------|-----------|--------------------|----------------|
| **Customer receives** | A monetary credit to their account | Loyalty points in the issuer's rewards program | A digital or physical voucher | A tag used for customer segmentation, rather than a direct reward | A higher earning rate on the standard rewards program | Loyalty points in a partner's rewards program |
| **Depends on** | Offer Calculation (Flat / Percentage / Slab) | Offer Calculation (Points Formula) | A predefined voucher or coupon | No additional reward value calculation | An existing base rewards program | An existing partner points integration |
| **Best for** | Direct monetary incentives | Driving engagement with the issuer's loyalty program | Campaign-specific redeemable rewards | Customer segmentation and future targeting | Increasing the earning rate of an existing rewards program | Cross-promoting a partner or co-branded rewards program |

<Note>
Available Outcome Types may vary based on the configured environment. Some outcome types may appear as **Coming Soon** and are not available for selection until they are enabled.
</Note>

---
## Cashback
Cashback credits a predefined monetary amount or percentage of the eligible transaction value to the customer's account after the configured offer conditions are successfully met.
**When to use it**
Choose **Cashback** when the objective is to provide a direct monetary incentive. It is well suited for campaigns that encourage customer spending across cards, UPI, or CASA-linked transactions.
**Example**
A bank wants credit card customers to earn **5% cashback** on fuel transactions. Select **Cashback** as the Outcome Type, then configure the reward calculation under **Offer Calculation**.
---
## Reward Points
Reward Points award loyalty points to customers after they successfully satisfy the configured offer conditions. The awarded points can be accumulated and redeemed through the issuer's rewards program.
**When to use it**
Choose **Reward Points** when the objective is to increase engagement with an existing loyalty program rather than provide a direct monetary reward.
**Example**
A bank wants premium credit card customers to earn **2 reward points for every ₹100 spent** on dining. Select **Reward Points** as the Outcome Type, then configure the points calculation under **Offer Calculation**.
---
## Voucher
Voucher issues a digital or physical voucher to eligible customers after the configured offer conditions are successfully satisfied. The voucher can then be redeemed according to the campaign rules.
**When to use it**
Choose **Voucher** when the reward is intended to be a one-time redeemable benefit, such as an onboarding incentive or a partner-brand promotion.
**Example**
A bank wants to reward customers who open a new savings account with a **₹500 shopping voucher**. Select **Voucher** as the Outcome Type.
---
## Smart Tag
Smart Tag assigns a predefined tag to customers after they qualify for an offer. These tags can be used for customer segmentation and to enable targeted campaigns in the future.
**When to use it**
Choose **Smart Tag** when the objective is customer identification rather than issuing a reward. Smart Tags help create customer segments that can later be used in **Offer User Base** for targeted campaigns.
**Example**
A bank wants to identify customers who spend more than **₹1,00,000 in a quarter** to target them with a future premium campaign. Select **Smart Tag** as the Outcome Type and configure the tag that should be assigned upon qualification.

---
## What Happens After Configuration?

After an Outcome Type is selected, the dashboard proceeds to the **Offer Scope** configuration. The selected Outcome Type remains fixed for the offer and determines the reward-related configurations available later in **Offer Calculation** and **Posting**.

## Which Outcome Type Should You Select?

<Tip>

- Select **Cashback** to reward customers with a direct monetary credit.
- Select **Reward Points** to award points through the issuer's loyalty program.
- Select **Voucher** to issue a redeemable voucher or coupon.
- Select **Smart Tag** to segment qualifying customers without issuing a monetary reward.
- Select **Accelerated Reward** to increase the earning rate of an existing rewards program.
- Select **Partner Points** to award points through a partner loyalty program.

</Tip>
